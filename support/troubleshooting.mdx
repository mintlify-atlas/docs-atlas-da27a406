## Quick Fixes

Most connection issues can be resolved with these simple steps. Try these first before contacting support.

<Warning>
**Before you start:** Never reset your router to factory settings unless instructed by our support team. This will erase your configuration and may require a technician visit.
</Warning>

## Internet Connection Issues

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="No Internet Connection">
    If you have no internet connection, follow these steps:

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Check physical connections">
        - Ensure the fiber optic cable is securely connected to your router
        - Check that the power adapter is plugged in and the router lights are on
        - Look for the PON or LOS light - it should be solid green (not red or blinking)
      </Step>
      
      <Step title="Restart your router">
        - Unplug the power cable from the router
        - Wait 30 seconds
        - Plug it back in and wait 2-3 minutes for it to fully restart
        - Check if the connection is restored
      </Step>
      
      <Step title="Check for service outages">
        - Contact us via WhatsApp to check if there's a known outage in your area
        - We'll notify you of the estimated restoration time
      </Step>
      
      <Step title="Test with wired connection">
        - Connect a device directly to the router using an Ethernet cable
        - If this works, the issue may be with your WiFi (see WiFi issues below)
      </Step>
    </Steps>

    <Note>
If none of these steps work, contact our 24/7 support team via WhatsApp or phone for immediate assistance.
    </Note>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Slow Internet Speeds">
    If your internet is slower than expected:

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Test your speed">
        - Visit [speedtest.net](https://speedtest.net) on a wired connection
        - Compare results to your plan speed (300/600/1000 Mbps)
        - Run the test multiple times for accuracy
      </Step>
      
      <Step title="Check connected devices">
        - Too many devices can slow your network
        - Disconnect devices you're not using
        - Check if any device is downloading large files
      </Step>
      
      <Step title="Restart your router">
        - Unplug the router for 30 seconds
        - Plug it back in and wait for full restart
        - Test speed again after restart
      </Step>
      
      <Step title="Test on different devices">
        - Try the speed test on multiple devices
        - If only one device is slow, the issue may be with that device
      </Step>
    </Steps>

    <Warning>
**WiFi vs Wired:** WiFi speeds are typically 60-80% of your plan speed. For maximum performance, use a wired Ethernet connection.
    </Warning>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Intermittent Connection Drops">
    If your connection keeps dropping:

    **Possible causes:**
    - Loose fiber cable connection
    - Router overheating
    - Firmware issues
    - WiFi interference (if wireless)

    **Solutions:**
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Check cable connections">
        Ensure all cables are firmly connected and not damaged
      </Step>
      
      <Step title="Check router placement">
        - Ensure router is in a well-ventilated area
        - Don't place in enclosed spaces or near heat sources
        - Keep vents clear of dust
      </Step>
      
      <Step title="Update router firmware">
        Contact support to check if a firmware update is available for your router
      </Step>
      
      <Step title="Monitor the pattern">
        - Note when drops occur (time of day, during specific activities)
        - Share this information with support for faster diagnosis
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## WiFi Issues

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Weak WiFi Signal / Dead Zones">
    If you have poor WiFi coverage in some areas:

    **Immediate fixes:**
    - Move router to a central location in your home
    - Place router on a high shelf (not on the floor)
    - Keep away from walls, metal objects, and other electronics
    - Ensure router antennas are pointing upward

    **Long-term solutions:**
    <CardGroup cols={2}>
      <Card title="Upgrade to Hogar+" icon="signal">
        Includes 1 Mesh repeater for extended coverage
      </Card>
      
      <Card title="Upgrade to Pro Gaming" icon="tower-broadcast">
        Includes WiFi 6E + 2 Mesh repeaters for maximum coverage
      </Card>
    </CardGroup>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can't Connect to WiFi">
    If devices can't connect to your WiFi:

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Check WiFi is enabled">
        - Look for WiFi lights on your router (should be solid or blinking)
        - Check if your device's WiFi is turned on
      </Step>
      
      <Step title="Verify password">
        - The default WiFi password is on a sticker on the router
        - If you changed it, try the new password
        - Passwords are case-sensitive
      </Step>
      
      <Step title="Forget and reconnect">
        - On your device, forget the WiFi network
        - Search for networks again
        - Select your network and enter the password
      </Step>
      
      <Step title="Restart both devices">
        - Restart your router (unplug for 30 seconds)
        - Restart the device that can't connect
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="WiFi Interference">
    If you experience WiFi issues that come and go:

    **Common sources of interference:**
    - Microwave ovens
    - Bluetooth devices
    - Baby monitors
    - Neighbor's WiFi networks
    - Cordless phones

    **Solutions:**
    - Move router away from interfering devices
    - Change WiFi channel (contact support for assistance)
    - Upgrade to WiFi 6 or 6E (less interference, included in higher plans)
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Router Issues

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Router Lights Meaning">
    Understanding your router's indicator lights:

    | Light | Color | Meaning |
    |-------|-------|----------|
    | Power | Green (solid) | Router is powered on |
    | Power | Off | No power - check power cable |
    | PON/LOS | Green (solid) | Fiber connection OK |
    | PON/LOS | Red or blinking | Fiber connection issue |
    | Internet | Green (solid) | Internet connected |
    | Internet | Red or off | No internet connection |
    | WiFi | Green (blinking) | WiFi active and transmitting |
    | LAN | Green (blinking) | Ethernet device connected |

    <Warning>
If the PON/LOS light is red or blinking, there may be an issue with your fiber connection. Contact support immediately.
    </Warning>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Router Overheating">
    Routers can overheat if not properly ventilated:

    **Signs of overheating:**
    - Router feels very hot to touch
    - Frequent disconnections
    - Slow performance

    **Prevention:**
    - Keep in well-ventilated area
    - Don't cover or stack items on top
    - Keep vents clear of dust
    - Don't place in direct sunlight
    - Consider using a small fan nearby in hot climates
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Forgot WiFi Password">
    If you forgot your WiFi password:

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Check router sticker">
        The default WiFi name and password are printed on a sticker on the router
      </Step>
      
      <Step title="Check connected device">
        If you have a device already connected, you can view the saved password in its settings
      </Step>
      
      <Step title="Contact support">
        Our support team can help you access your router settings or reset the password if needed
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Gaming & Streaming Issues

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="High Ping / Latency in Games">
    For the best gaming experience:

    **Recommended:**
    - Use wired Ethernet connection (not WiFi)
    - Close background apps and downloads
    - Upgrade to Pro Gaming plan for lowest latency

    **Troubleshooting:**
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Test your ping">
        Use speedtest.net to check your ping (should be under 20ms)
      </Step>
      
      <Step title="Check QoS settings">
        Contact support to enable Quality of Service (QoS) for gaming traffic priority
      </Step>
      
      <Step title="Use 5GHz WiFi band">
        If you must use WiFi, connect to the 5GHz band for lower latency
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Buffering While Streaming">
    If videos buffer frequently:

    - Check your speed (may need to upgrade plan)
    - Close other applications using bandwidth
    - Use wired connection for 4K streaming
    - Restart your streaming device
    - Check if multiple people are streaming simultaneously

    **Recommended speeds for streaming:**
    - HD (1080p): 5 Mbps per stream
    - 4K: 25 Mbps per stream
    - Multiple 4K streams: Hogar+ (600 Mbps) or Pro Gaming (1000 Mbps)
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
