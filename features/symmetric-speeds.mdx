## How Fiber Enables Symmetry

### The Technical Difference

**Cable/DSL (Asymmetric)**
- Uses frequency division - different frequencies for upload/download
- Upload gets small portion of available spectrum
- Physical limitations prevent symmetric speeds

**Fiber Optic (Symmetric)**
- Uses wavelength division - separate light wavelengths for each direction
- Upload and download use dedicated wavelengths
- No physical reason to limit upload speeds

<Info>
  Telenet's fiber network is designed with symmetric capacity from the beginning. We don't artificially limit upload speeds like cable providers do.
</Info>

## Measuring Your Symmetric Speed

Verify you're getting full symmetric speeds:

1. **Use our web portal**: Built-in speed test shows both directions
2. **Wired test recommended**: Connect via Ethernet for most accurate results
3. **Check both directions**: Test download, then upload
4. **Multiple tests**: Run tests at different times of day

<Note>
  WiFi speeds may be lower than wired speeds due to wireless overhead. For full symmetric speeds, use Ethernet connections when possible.
</Note>
