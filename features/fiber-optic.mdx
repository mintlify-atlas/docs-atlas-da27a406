## What is Fiber Optic Internet?

Telenet uses **pure fiber optic technology** to deliver internet directly to your home. Unlike traditional cable or copper connections, fiber optics use light signals through glass fibers to transmit data at incredible speeds.

## Fiber vs. Traditional Cable

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Speed Consistency">
    **Fiber**: Maintains full speed 24/7, regardless of network congestion
    
    **Cable**: Speeds fluctuate during peak hours when neighbors are online
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Upload Performance">
    **Fiber**: Symmetric speeds - upload matches download speed
    
    **Cable**: Severely limited upload speeds (often 10x slower than download)
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Signal Degradation">
    **Fiber**: Light signals travel long distances without quality loss
    
    **Cable**: Electrical signals degrade over distance, requiring boosters
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Weather Resistance">
    **Fiber**: Immune to electromagnetic interference and moisture
    
    **Cable**: Susceptible to water damage and electrical interference
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Why Telenet Uses Pure Fiber

<Info>
  All Telenet plans include **fibra óptica pura** - there are no shared speeds or hybrid fiber-coax connections. You get dedicated fiber bandwidth from our network directly to your home.
</Info>

From the network backbone to your router, every inch is fiber optic cable. This means:

- **No bottlenecks**: Your connection isn't slowed by copper "last-mile" infrastructure
- **Maximum reliability**: Fewer connection points mean fewer potential failure points
- **True symmetric speeds**: Upload and download use the same fiber pathway

## Technical Benefits

### Bandwidth Capacity

Fiber optic cables can carry significantly more data than copper alternatives:

- **Copper (DSL)**: ~100 Mbps maximum
- **Cable (DOCSIS 3.1)**: Up to 1 Gbps shared among neighborhood
- **Fiber Optic**: Multi-gigabit capacity per household

<Tip>
  Even on our Básico plan at 300 Mbps, you're using only a fraction of the fiber's total capacity - meaning we can easily upgrade your speed without changing any physical infrastructure.
</Tip>

### Latency Performance

Fiber optics transmit data at approximately 70% the speed of light, resulting in:

- **Gaming**: Sub-10ms ping to local servers
- **Video Calls**: No lag or echo during conferences
- **Streaming**: Instant buffering and seamless 4K playback

### Installation & Service Delivery

<Note>

The installation process includes:

4. Testing full speed delivery to ensure quality

## Future-Proof Technology

Fiber optic infrastructure is built to last decades:

- **Scalability**: Easily upgraded to multi-gigabit speeds through equipment updates
- **Longevity**: Fiber cables have 50+ year lifespan vs. 15-20 years for copper
- **New Services**: Ready for emerging technologies like 8K streaming, VR/AR, and cloud gaming

<Tip>
  When you choose Telenet fiber, you're investing in technology that won't become obsolete. As internet demands grow, your connection can grow with them.
</Tip>
